Chapter 1: The Ironclad Rule

This is a world where magic flows through the veins of every living being. Some people have it like an overflowing river, others like a leaky faucet, but the ironclad rule is this: everyone has it. And in a world where magic is currency, status, and law all rolled into one, those born with barely a drop are naturally looked down upon.

Asahi wakes with a jolt to the sound of his father hollering.

"Asahi! Hurry up n' wake up! The cows ain't gon' milk themselves!"

Still half-asleep, he rubs his eyes and stumbles to the back of the hut where the cows lazily chew on grass. Bucket in hand, he starts milking. Just as the rhythm sets in, squish. Something unpleasant wiggles under his bare foot. His eyes flick down. Fresh cow dung. Of course.

He sighed, wiping his foot on the dry grass. Another day, another reminder of his place at the very bottom of a world built on magic. The other village children would be practicing elemental manipulation or healing spells by now, while he was stuck with chores that even the simplest magic could accomplish in seconds.

As he continued milking, he couldn't help but glance toward the distant spires of the Mage Academy visible on the horizon. A place he could only dream of, reserved for those with magic strong enough to be measured. His own magical signature was so faint that the village elder had declared him "practically null" during the annual testing ceremony.

Little did he know that today would be different. Today, everything would change.
